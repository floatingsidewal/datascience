# Deep Learning & Neural Networks

Graduate programs in Deep Learning focus on multi-layer neural network architectures including CNNs, RNNs, transformers, and attention mechanisms. These programs prepare students for advanced research and industry roles in AI.

---

## What This Subdiscipline Studies

Deep Learning is a subset of machine learning that uses artificial neural networks with multiple layers to learn hierarchical representations from data. Graduate programs cover:

- **Neural Network Fundamentals**: Perceptrons, activation functions, backpropagation, optimization
- **Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs)**: Image classification, object detection, segmentation
- **Recurrent Neural Networks (RNNs)**: Sequence modeling, LSTMs, GRUs, time series
- **Transformer Architectures**: Self-attention, multi-head attention, positional encoding
- **Generative Models**: GANs, VAEs, diffusion models, autoregressive generation
- **Optimization & Regularization**: SGD variants, batch normalization, dropout, weight decay
- **Hardware & Systems**: GPU computing, distributed training, model compression

---

## Real-World Applications

### Speech Recognition
Voice assistants like Siri, Alexa, and Google Assistant use deep learning for speech-to-text conversion, natural language understanding, and voice synthesis.

### Image & Video Understanding
Social media platforms use deep learning for content moderation, face recognition, photo organization, and video recommendation systems.

### Drug Discovery
Pharmaceutical companies employ deep learning to predict molecular properties, identify drug candidates, and model protein structures (AlphaFold).

### Autonomous Systems
Self-driving vehicles, drones, and robots use deep neural networks for perception, planning, and control in complex environments.

### Content Generation
Text-to-image models, language models, and creative AI tools generate art, music, code, and written content.
