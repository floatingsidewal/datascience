# Business Analytics & Predictive Modeling

Graduate programs in Business Analytics apply statistical and machine learning techniques to business problems including forecasting, customer analytics, and data-driven decision making.

---

## What This Subdiscipline Studies

Business Analytics bridges the gap between data science and business strategy. Graduate programs cover:

- **Descriptive Analytics**: Data visualization, dashboards, KPI tracking, reporting
- **Predictive Analytics**: Forecasting, regression modeling, classification, time series
- **Prescriptive Analytics**: Optimization, simulation, decision analysis
- **Customer Analytics**: Segmentation, churn prediction, lifetime value modeling
- **Marketing Analytics**: A/B testing, attribution modeling, campaign optimization
- **Operations Analytics**: Supply chain optimization, demand forecasting, inventory management
- **Financial Analytics**: Risk modeling, fraud detection, credit scoring

---

## Real-World Applications

### Retail & E-Commerce
Predictive models forecast demand, optimize pricing, personalize recommendations, and manage inventory. Analytics drive decisions from store layout to supply chain logistics.

### Financial Services
Banks use predictive models for credit scoring, fraud detection, algorithmic trading, and customer churn prediction. Risk models inform lending decisions and regulatory compliance.

### Healthcare Administration
Analytics optimize hospital operations, predict patient readmissions, manage staffing levels, and identify cost reduction opportunities.

### Manufacturing
Predictive maintenance prevents equipment failures, quality analytics catch defects early, and demand forecasting optimizes production planning.

### Marketing & Advertising
Attribution models measure campaign effectiveness, customer segmentation enables personalization, and predictive models identify high-value prospects.
